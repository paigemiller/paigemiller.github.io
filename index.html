Ecology/Statistics/Computational Epidemiology/Population Dynamics

I am interested in using statistical and mathematical models to explore the ecological, evolutionary, and epidemiological processes driving infectious disease dynamics. My research questions range from deriving theoretical insights into the dynamics of pathogen transmission in heterogeneous populations to applied questions regarding infectious disease surveillance and intervention strategies.

Current Projects

Brief descriptions of my current research.

Spatial patterns of an infectious disease on the verge of elimination.

Disease transmission is an inherently spatial process. How do spatial signatures of incidence data differ when a disease is almost eliminated? We are using a simulation-based approach to answer this question. Stay tuned!

Early warning signals for disease elimination in agent-based models.

Statistical signatures of time series change prior to disease elimination. These signatures are theoretically expected in SIR models due to critical slowing down . What we don't know is how, and if, these signals will change when the model is more complex, and the signal is increasingly spread among more compartments.

TB transmission along a network of individuals in Uganda.

How do the social networks of TB-positive individuals differ from those of TB-negative individuals? Why are males more than twice as likely to be TB-positive? My projects will focus on answering these questions.

Past Research

Brief descriptions of, and links to, publications from my past research.

The effects of seasonality on forecasting infectious disease emergence.

As a graduate student at UGA, I studied how seasonality in disease transmission impacts our ability to forecast if disease emergence is likely. I'll post a link once it is accepted!

The effects of brief messaging about undiagnosed infections on HIV testing.

While serving as an intern at CDC, I assisted with a study to quantitatively assess how a brief message regarding HIV testing and unknown infections among black and Latino MSM impacts behavioral intent for HIV testing and condom use. A link to the publication can be found here.

The imacts of age on reproduction.

As an undergraduate at Gustavus Adolphus College, I worked in the Bloch-Qazi Lab and studied how female age influences fertility in Drosophila melanogaster. A link to a review we wrote as a class project can be found here.
