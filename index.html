Ecology/Statistics/Computational Epidemiology/Population Dynamics

I am interested in using statistical and mathematical models to explore the social, ecological, and epidemiological processes driving infectious disease dynamics. My research interests range from studying the dynamics of pathogen transmission in networks to applied questions regarding infectious disease surveillance and intervention strategies.

Current Projects

Tuberculosis transmission and social network structure in Uganda.

In most countries, more cases of TB are reported among men than women. Few studies have investigated the role social network structure may play in determining male-bias, particularly in regions most burdened by TB. To see what aspects of network structure may affect TB transmission and male-bias in particular, we're analyzing features of a large social network dataset from Kampala, Uganda. In prep for publication at Int. J. Epidemiol.

Assortativity and sex-bias in TB.

Infectious diseases rarely affect males and females equally. This is true for human and animal pathogens with all kinds of transmission modes and across pathogen types. In many cases, differences in immunity can account for observed sex-bias. In other cases, such as TB, evidence suggests differences in pathogen exposure exacerbate sex-bias. To see the relative impact of individual-level differences in infection and population-level preferential mixing by sex on sex-bias, we are using a simulation-based approach.

Spread of SARS-CoV-2 in structured populations.

We're developing theory for how SARS-CoV-2 spreads in structured, high transmission settings, such as nursing homes and prisons. We're also comparing outbreaks in the US to help understand why the extent and intensity of transmission varies across different settings. We hope to help guide where more restrictions are needed or where more close surveillance is required to avoid a resurgence in transmission.

Past Research

The effects of seasonality on forecasting infectious disease emergence.

As a graduate student at UGA, I studied how seasonality in disease transmission impacts our ability to forecast if disease emergence is likely. Check it out!

The effects of brief messaging about undiagnosed infections on HIV testing.

While serving as an intern at CDC, I assisted with a study to quantitatively assess how a brief message regarding HIV testing and unknown infections among black and Latino MSM impacts behavioral intent for HIV testing and condom use. A link to the publication can be found here.

The imacts of age on reproduction.

As an undergraduate at Gustavus Adolphus College, I worked in the Bloch-Qazi Lab and studied how female age influences fertility in Drosophila melanogaster. A link to a review we wrote as a class project can be found here and a link to our research paper can be found here.
